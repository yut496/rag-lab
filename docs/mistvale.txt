Mistvale is a small mountain town with a population of about 4,200 people.
It was founded in 1887 by wool traders.
The town is famous for its annual Lantern Festival, held every September.
The Mistvale River runs through the center of town and powers an old mill.
The highest point nearby is Mount Corrin, which rises to 2,100 meters.
Mistvale is known for its hand-woven wool blankets, sold at the weekend market.
The town has no traffic lights and only one bridge across the river.
