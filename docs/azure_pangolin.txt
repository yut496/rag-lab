The azure pangolin is a nocturnal mammal native to the Karoo highlands.
Its body is covered in blue-tinged keratin scales that overlap like roof tiles.
It feeds almost entirely on termites, eating up to 20,000 in a single night.
A female gives birth to one pup per year, which rides on her tail.
An adult azure pangolin weighs around 8 kilograms.
The species is critically endangered, with fewer than 900 left in the wild.
When threatened, it rolls into a tight ball that predators cannot open.
